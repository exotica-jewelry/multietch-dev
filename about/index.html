About Multi‑Etch®

Optimize titanium for welding and anodizing without hydrofluoric acid! #

After using hydrofluoric acid ourselves for 10 years, we understood the hazards of working with HF and nitric acids. Read more about our history.

Even relatively “safe” consumer products such as rust removers, which are sometimes used for etching titanium, contain hydrofluoric acid. Read more about the environmental and safety issues of HF.

Etch titanium safely with Multi‑Etch #

  1. Mix the Multi‑Etch: Shipped to you as a dry powder for ease of storage. Just add water!

  2. Etch the titanium: Just 7–10 seconds in heated Multi‑Etch does the job.

  3. Enjoy clean, bright titanium: Anodize as usual for brilliant colors!

Use Multi‑Etch® on many different metals #

Whether you’re preparing a metal for electrical anodizing or cleaning a metal for welding and other uses, Multi‑Etch® will do the job! Zirconium, niobium, platinum, copper, NiTinol, aluminum and more all respond well to Multi‑Etch®. See our etch rate tables for all metals.

Safe to use #

Per the CDC, “Even small splashes of high-concentration hydrogen fluoride products on the skin can be fatal.”

While we recommend using gloves, if Multi‑Etch gets on your skin you can simply wash it off with no ill effects.

Go green #

Unlike hydrofluoric acid, fluorides in Multi‑Etch are bound up in solution and never released as a gas.

In many municipalities, spent Multi‑Etch can be neutralized with baking soda and poured down the drain.
